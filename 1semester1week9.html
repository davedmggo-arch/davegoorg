Week 9: Geometric Refinement & The Hero Asset

This week, we are moving away from "placeholder" boxes and into hard-surface modeling. We will be working directly on our Master Assets at the world origin so that our chosen room layout updates in real-time.

Start Learning

Key Concepts

Step 1: Refining the Architectural Shell

    Now that the layout is set, the room needs to feel like a real structure, not just a hollow box.

    • Adding Thickness: Give your walls and floor some "weight." Use the Extrude tool or a Solidify Modifier to ensure your walls aren't infinitely thin.

    • Frames and Moldings: * Replace your window and door "planes" with actual geometry.

      • Add a simple Baseboard where the walls meet the floor and a Window Sill. These small details catch the light and are essential for "Design Unity."

    • The Door & Window: Model a simple frame around the openings you designated in Week 1.

Step 2: From Block-out to Functional Furniture

    Return to your Master Collections at $(0,0,0)$. We are going to add "functional" detail to our low and mid-level props.

    • The Bed: Add a "lip" to the bed frame. Use the Bevel Tool (Ctrl + B) on the edges of your mattress and pillows to break those sharp, computer-generated corners.

    • The Desk & Cabinet: Add drawers and handles. Instead of a solid block, ensure the desktop has a realistic thickness and the cabinet doors have visible seams.

    • The Chair: Refine the legs and the backrest. If it's an office chair, model the "wheels" as simple cylinders to provide a recognizable silhouette.

Step 3: The "Hero" Prop - The Desktop Computer

    This is where you show off your precision. While the bed is a large, simple form, the computer should be dense with detail.

    • The Tower: Don't just leave it as a rectangle. Model the Front Grill, the Power Button, and the IO Ports (USB/Headphone jacks) on the top or front.

    • The Monitor: Create a clear distinction between the Screen, the Bezel, and the Stand. Use Inset (I) and Extrude (E) to create the recessed screen area.

    • The Keyboard: This is the ultimate test of your spacing. You don't need to model every internal switch, but I want to see individual Keycaps or at least rows of keys defined by geometry, not just a flat texture.

Step 4: UV Unwrapping & Texel Density

    Before we can add materials in Week 3, we must "flatten" our 3D objects so textures can sit on them correctly.

    • Seam Placement: Place your seams where they are naturally hidden (e.g., the bottom of the desk, the back of the computer).

    • The Smart UV Project: For complex shapes like the computer, you can start with Smart UV Project, but you must manually clean up any overlapping islands.

    • Screen Focus: Ensure the Monitor Screen and Computer Case have clean, straight UV layouts. This will be vital when we add glowing screen textures and logos next week.

Weekly Checklist

  • [ ] Walls, floor, and ceiling have realistic thickness.

  • [ ] All Master Assets at $(0,0,0)$ have been refined with bevels and extra geometry.

  • [ ] The Desktop Computer (Hero Prop) has distinct ports, buttons, and keys.

  • [ ] All objects have been UV Unwrapped with no visible stretching.

  • [ ] All new objects follow the SM_Name naming convention.
