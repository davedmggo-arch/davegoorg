Project Brief: Beyond the Horizon

Students will create a vast exterior environment that houses their character and procedural assets. This project focuses on Cinematic Composition—the art of framing a scene to tell a story. You will learn to expand your world using sky boxes and matte paintings to create an illusion of infinite distance without sacrificing software performance.

Core Requirements

  • Scale: Establish clear foreground, midground, and background layers.
  • Atmosphere: Utilize lighting temperature and exposure to set the narrative mood.
  • Integration: Successfully place Project 2’s character and Project 1’s assets into a cohesive story beat.

Deliverables

  • Exterior Environment: A fully integrated 3D scene with sky box and matte painting elements.
  • Cinematic Renders: A series of high-quality shots showcasing different narrative angles.
  • Shot Breakdown: Documentation explaining your composition choices (Rule of Thirds, Leading Lines).
  • Portfolio Package: A professional presentation including an artist statement and process work.

Best Practices

  • Depth of Field: Use subtle camera blur to separate your character from the background and focus viewer attention.
  • Atmospheric Perspective: Use fog or volume scattering to make distant objects appear lighter and cooler in color.
  • Visual Guidance: Use environment assets (like roads or fallen debris) as leading lines that point toward your focal point.
