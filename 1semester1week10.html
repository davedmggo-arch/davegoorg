Week 10: Advanced Character: Retopology

You will convert your heavy sculpt into a "light" production mesh, ensuring the edge flow allows for perfect deformation at the joints.

Start Learning

Learning Outcomes

  • Convert a high-density full-body sculpt into a clean, low-poly mesh optimized for professional animation.
  • Implement the "Rule of Three" at major joints to ensure smooth mesh deformation during movement.
  • Design efficient edge flow around the eyes, mouth, and hands to support complex facial expressions and finger poses.

Key Concepts

1. The Full-Body Retopology Workflow

This week is the most technical phase of the project. You will use your high-poly body sculpt as a "live" template and draw a new, simplified mesh directly over its surface.

  • Manual Edge Placement: Instead of using automated tools, you will manually place vertices to ensure the "edge flow" follows the natural contours and muscle lines of the body.
  • Polygon Economy: Your goal is to use the fewest polygons possible while maintaining the character's silhouette. Focus density on the face and hands, while keeping larger, simpler polygons for the torso and legs.
  • Symmetry: Use the Mirror Modifier to retopologize only one side of the body. Once finished, you will "Apply" the modifier to create a single, seamless object.

2. Edge Flow & Deformation

The "flow" of your edges determines how the character will look when they move. A mesh with poor topology will "collapse" or "pinch" when the character bends an arm or leg.

  • Joint Loops: You must ensure there are concentric loops of polygons around major joints like the shoulders, elbows, and knees.
  • The Rule of Three: To ensure smooth bending, always use at least three edge loops at every joint—one for the center of the bend and two "supporting" loops on either side.
  • Facial Loops: Revisit the facial loops learned in Project 2. Ensure they circle the eyes and mouth in a way that allows for complex smiles, frowns, and blinks.

3. Maintaining Professional Standards

For an advanced character, the quality of your wireframe is just as important as the look of the sculpt.

  • 100% Quads: Every face on your model should be a four-sided polygon (quad). Avoid triangles and N-gons, as they cause ugly shadows and rendering errors.
  • Uniform Face Size: Try to keep your polygon sizes relatively consistent across the body. This prevents issues when you move into the UV unwrapping and texturing phase next week.
  • Snapping: Keep Face Snapping enabled so your new vertices stay perfectly glued to the surface of the high-poly sculpt.

4. Essential Shortcuts & Tools

  • Poly Build Tool: Found in the left-hand toolbar, this tool allows you to click and drag new faces into existence instantly.
  • Fill Face (F): Select two edges and press F to bridge them with a new face.
  • Loop Cut (Ctrl + R): Add extra resolution exactly where a joint needs more flexibility.
  • Auto-Merge: Enable this in the top-right options to snap vertices together automatically when they get close to each other.
  • X-Ray Mode (Alt + Z): Essential for seeing your high-poly sculpt "through" your new low-poly mesh as you work.

5. Best Practices for Technical Mesh Design

  • Check Your Normals: Frequently check that all your faces are pointing outward. If they face inward, the model will look "broken" during texturing.
  • Isolate Areas: Use the H key to hide parts of your high-poly sculpt that are in the way (like hiding one leg while working on the inner thigh of the other).
  • Relaxing Geometry: Use the Smooth tool in Edit Mode to even out the spacing of your vertices, which helps create a cleaner wireframe.

6. Week 10 Assignment

  • The Task: Submit the low-poly retopologized character.
  • Step 1: Create a complete quad-based mesh over your high-poly full-body sculpt.
  • Step 2: Ensure proper "Rule of Three" loops are present at the elbows, knees, and shoulders.
  • Step 3: Finalize the facial edge loops to support future expressions.
  • Submit: A screenshot of your character in "Wireframe Overlay" mode showing the front and back topology.
