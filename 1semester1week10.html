Week 10: Finishing Touches & Atmosphere

In our final lesson, we add the subtle details and lighting that transform a 3D model into a believable, lived-in story.

Step 1: Set Dressing & Color Focus

Set dressing involves adding "clutter" that makes a room feel inhabited. For this project, these objects should be very low-detail. Think beveled cubes or simple cylinders.

  • The Minimalist Approach: Add items like books on the shelves, a notebook on the desk, or a few pens.

  • Detail Level: These should be no more complex than a cube with a small Bevel to catch the light.

  • Color as a Tool: Use color to guide the eye. If your room is mostly neutral, a single bright red book or a colorful poster can create a Central Focus for the viewer.

  • Consistency Check: Ensure all props from previous weeks are colored and maintain the mid-level detail we established in Week 2.

Step 2: Acting Space & Movement

Consider how a character would actually exist in this room. The arrangement of your set dressing should reflect their personality and physical limitations.

  • Functional Paths: Ensure there is enough "Acting Space" for a character to walk from the door to the bed or sit at the desk.

  • Limiting Movement: Use your furniture to "corral" the character. For example, a cabinet placed near the bed might limit their movement, creating a cramped, cozy feel.

Environment Art

Now we set the mood. We want two primary light sources and one "focal" source.

  • Main Illumination: Use the Window and the Ceiling Light as your primary sources.

  • The Focal Point: Use the Computer Monitor as a light source to draw the viewer's attention directly to your hero setup.

  • Volumetric God Rays: Use the Principled Volume Material tool in the Work Helpers script. This tool automatically converts a shape into a Volumetric Field.

  • Atmospheric Perspective: When light passes through this volumetric field, it creates "god rays" and a sense of depth, making the air in the room feel tangible.

Step 4: Rendering From Eevee to Cycles

While Eevee provides great real-time feedback, we will use Cycles for our final submission to achieve maximum light accuracy and realistic reflections.

  • Switching Engines: Go to the Render Properties tab and switch from Eevee to Cycles.

  • Backface Culling: Ensure your interior walls are properly configured using the script's Flip Normals & Apply Mat tool so the camera can see into the room.

  • Denoising & Samples: To keep render times low while maintaining quality, enable the Denoise feature in the Render tab.

  • Render Limit: Set your Max Samples to 40. This is more than enough when combined with denoising to get a clean, professional result quickly.

Submission Guidelines

Your final project must be organized into clear collections. Ensure you run the Setup Eevee & Pack tool one last time to embed all your textures into the file.

Final Submission Checklist

  • Props: All 8 props at mid-level detail, fully colored, with minimal set dressing added.

  • Atmosphere: Volumetric god rays active and lighting focused on the hero prop.

  • Organization: Clean collection hierarchy with all objects named correctly using the SM_ prefix.

  • Renders: 3 final PNG images rendered in Cycles at 40 samples.
