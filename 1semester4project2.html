Project 2: The Narrative Inhabitant (Weeks 4-8)

Having established the world, we now create the life that lives within it. This project focuses on Character Expansion, moving from high-concept sculpting to technical execution and rigging.

Project Brief: Character Expansion

Students will design and model a character that fits the theme established in Project 1. The goal is to produce an "animation-ready" model. This means clean topology, logical joint placement, and a functional skeletal rig. You will also produce professional technical specifications and turnaround sheets.

Design Constraints

  • Polygon Limit: Maximum 5,000 polygons for the final optimized mesh.
  • Stylization: No fur or hair elements; focus on form, silhouette, and volume.
  • Technical: Must include a basic body rig (no facial rigging required).

Deliverables

  • Character Model: Optimized mesh with animation-ready topology.
  • Turnaround Sheets: Front, Side, Back, and 3/4 views.
  • Technical Specs: Documentation detailing model requirements and component breakdown.
  • Posed Renders: The character posed within the Project 1 environment.

Best Practices

  • Silhouette Check: Toggle X-Ray or Flat Shading frequently to ensure the character's pose and form are readable at a glance.
  • Anatomical Landmarks: Ensure bones are placed exactly at the pivot points of joints to prevent mesh "collapsing."
  • Weight Painting: Use smooth gradients in weight painting to allow for organic bending at the elbows and knees.
