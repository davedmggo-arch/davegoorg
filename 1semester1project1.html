Project 1: Table & Props (Weeks 1-3)

You will guide a high-quality table and three distinct props through the entire production pipeline, including modeling, UV unwrapping, and cinematic rendering.

Start Learning!!

Project Brief

Goal: You will create a high-quality 3D scene featuring a cleanly modeled table and three distinct props—a lamp base, a bookend, and a decorative item. You will guide these objects through the entire production pipeline: modeling, UV unwrapping, material application, and final cinematic rendering.

Deliverables:

  • 1 Blender File: FirstName_LastName_ANI106_Project_001_v001.blend
  • 3 JPEGs: FirstName_LastName_ANI106_Project_001_v001_001-003.jpg

Marking Rubric (Total: 100 Points):

  • Modeling & Topology (25 pts): Use of primary tools (Extrude, Bevel, Loop Cut) and maintenance of clean, quad-based topology.
  • UVs & Materials (25 pts): Quality of UV unwrapping (seam placement) and effective use of the Principled BSDF shader for realism.
  • Lighting & Composition (25 pts): Successful implementation of 3-point lighting and Rule-of-Thirds framing.
  • Organization & Technical (25 pts): Proper naming conventions, applied scales, and efficient mesh management in the Outliner.

Weekly Breakdown

Week 1

You will demystify the Blender interface and master primary mesh editing tools to begin the structural "blocking" of your table model.

Week 2

You will populate your scene with three distinct props while learning to "flatten" 3D objects through UV unwrapping to prepare them for textures.

Week 3

You will act as a cinematographer, implementing professional 3-point lighting and rule-of-thirds composition to render a high-quality portfolio piece.
