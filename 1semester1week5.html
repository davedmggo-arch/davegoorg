Week 5: Torso, Arms & Retopology

You will expand your sculpt to the torso and arms before performing retopology to create a clean mesh that the computer can easily animate.

Start Learning!!

Learning Outcomes

  • Extend a character sculpt from the head to the torso and arms while maintaining anatomical proportions.
  • Execute a manual retopology workflow to convert a high-poly sculpt into an animation-ready mesh.
  • Implement a basic bone armature and apply skin weights to test mesh deformation.

Key Concepts

1. Sculpting: Expanding to the Torso & Arms

This week, you will expand your head sculpt into a character bust, focusing on the upper body and anatomical flow.

  • The A-Pose: Sculpt the arms out to the side rather than straight down. This prevents the mesh from merging with the chest and makes the next technical steps much easier.
  • Blocking Volume: Focus on the large muscle groups—the pectorals, the trapezius, and the deltoids. Avoid detail until the chest and back look structurally sound.
  • Proportional Check: A standard character's torso is roughly 2.5 to 3 "heads" tall. Use this as a guide to ensure your character doesn't look too short or stretched.

2. Retopology: Rebuilding for Animation

High-poly sculpts contain millions of polygons, making them impossible to animate. Retopology is the process of drawing a new, low-poly mesh over the top of your sculpt.

  • Edge Loops: You must place rings of edges around the eyes, mouth, and joints. These loops allow the mesh to stretch and compress smoothly.
  • The Rule of Three: For areas that bend (like the elbow), use at least three edge loops. This provides enough "geometry" for the joint to bend without collapsing.
  • Polygon Economy: Keep your face count as low as possible while still maintaining the silhouette of your sculpt.

3. Rigging Basics: The Digital Skeleton

Once you have a clean mesh, you add an Armature (bones) to make it movable.

  • Bone Placement: Align the bones with the interior of your mesh. The "joint" of the bone should sit exactly where the real-world joint would be.
  • Parenting with Weights: Use Blender's "Automatic Weights" to quickly attach the mesh to the bones. This tells the mesh which parts should move when a bone is rotated.
  • Weight Painting: Use a brush to manually "paint" the influence of a bone. Red areas move 100% with the bone, while blue areas do not move at all.

4. Essential Shortcuts & Tools

  • Add Bone: Shift + A > Armature (in Object Mode).
  • Extrude Bone: Select a bone in Edit Mode and press E to grow a chain.
  • Snapping for Retopology: Enable "Face Project" snapping so your new vertices "stick" to the surface of the high-poly sculpt.
  • Parent Mesh to Rig: Select the Mesh, then the Rig, and press Ctrl + P > With Automatic Weights.
  • X-Ray Mode: Press Alt + Z to see your bones inside the mesh.

5. Week 5 Assignment

  • The Task: Create a retopologized bust with a simple rig.
  • Step 1: Complete the sculpt of the torso and arms in an A-pose.
  • Step 2: Create a clean, low-poly mesh over the head and torso using retopology tools.
  • Step 3: Add a simple bone chain for the neck and head and parent the mesh to it.
  • Submit: A screenshot of your clean retopologized mesh (wireframe) and a screenshot of the character in a simple "head tilt" pose.
