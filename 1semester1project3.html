Project 3: Interior Design (Weeks 7-8)

You will design an "acting space" interior that supports camera movement and utilizes procedural materials and advanced lighting to tell a story.
Start Learning!!

Project Brief

    This three-week project guides you through the professional pipeline of interior environment design, moving from structural "grey-boxing" to high-detail hard-surface modeling. By utilizing Collection Instances, you will learn a non-destructive workflow that allows them to manage and update assets across three distinct bedroom layouts simultaneously. The final goal is to synthesize modeling, lighting, and material skills into a cohesive, portfolio-ready render of a functional "acting space."

Project Grading Rubric

  • Technical Setup & Organization (25%)

    • Consistent use of Collection Instances for all 8 props to maintain a non-destructive workflow.

    • Strict adherence to the naming convention (SM_, M_, C_) and a clean, filtered Outliner.

    • Correct viewport configuration, including flipped normals and Backface Culling in the material settings.

  • Architectural & Spatial Logic (25%)

    • Accurate real-world scale for the room shell (Small, Medium, or Large) and all furniture.

    • Clean architectural cutouts for the window and door using the loop-cut and separate method.

    • Effective "Acting Space" design in the 3 iterations, ensuring clear flow and logical clearance for a character.

  • Modeling & UV Execution (25%)

    • Refining block-out shapes into final meshes with realistic thickness, bevels, and curved silhouettes.

    • High-level detail on the Desktop Computer (Hero Prop), featuring distinct keycaps, ports, and monitor details.

    • Clear, un-stretched UV unwrapping that effectively utilizes the 0-1 texture space for all master assets.

  • Look Development & Presentation (25%)

    • Application of PBR materials that accurately represent wood, fabric, metal, and plastic surfaces.

    • Mood-driven Eevee lighting setup, including a glowing Emissive computer screen and natural window light.

    • Professional final output consisting of a "Wide" layout shot and a "Hero" detail shot, named correctly according to the submission guidelines.

Featured Courses

Week 8: Spatial Design & Iteration

You will build their 8 core props as Master Assets at the world origin and instance them into three distinct room shells. This phase focuses on mastering real-world scale, backface culling, and non-destructive workflow organization.

Week 9: Hard-Surface Modeling

Students will transition from simple blocks to refined geometry by adding thickness to the room and bevels to the furniture. This stage emphasizes hard-surface modeling techniques to remove "computer-sharp" edges and add realistic detail.

Week 10: Camera Composition & Presentation

The project concludes with the setup of "Wide" and "Hero" cameras at human eye-level for a final professional presentation. Students will produce two high-quality renders that showcase both their spatial layout and their technical modeling prowess.
