Project 2: Fundamental Environment Design (Weeks 8–12)

You will design a modular 3D environment using subdivision modeling techniques. This project emphasizes building re-usable assets, applying materials, and telling a story through your environment. Your scene should include at least three modular structures and two detailed props, fully surfaced with materials, lit, and rendered.

Deliverables:

  • Three modular environment pieces (e.g., walls, arches, floors).
  • Two detailed props (e.g., furniture, machines, or decorative elements).
  • Applied materials with proper UVs and shaders.
  • At least three professional renders (wide, medium, close-up).
  • Documentation of modular workflow and material application.

Grading (50% of semester grade):

Technical Proficiency (35%) – Subdivision modeling, edge flow, UV unwrapping, optimization.
Artistic Direction (25%) – Strong composition, storytelling elements, visual quality.
Problem-Solving (25%) – Effective modular design, creative use of assets, innovative solutions.
Professional Presentation (15%) – Organized files, polished renders, clear documentation.

Week 8: Subdivision Surfaces, Edge Flow & Supporting Loops

Students move into Subdivision Surface (SubD) modeling: what it is, how to add/control it in Blender, and how edge flow and supporting loops/creases keep forms sharp and clean. The week ends with a guided SubD build (stylized chair) and a topology QA checklist.

Week 9: Material Fundamentals (Shader Editor, Principled BSDF, UVs & Texturing)

Students learn the Shader Editor (node-based materials), build a small material library with Principled BSDF, then UV unwrap and apply textures to subdivision models. By the end, they’ll create several clean materials, texture at least one prop, and document settings—skills required for the Project 2 final (materials contribute to the Technical Proficiency slice). 

Week 10: Modular Design Principles, Snap-to-Grid & Tile Systems

Students learn modular design (benefits, principles, planning), then execute snap-to-grid workflows and build both a tile set and a starter modular wall kit. Emphasis is on standard sizes, connection points, seamless alignment, and iteration. These skills directly support Project 2’s deliverables and grading (Technical Proficiency 35%).   

Week 11: Environment Assembly, Advanced Lighting & Multi-Camera Composition

Students bring their modular assets together to build a cohesive environment, apply advanced lighting (key/fill/accent + atmosphere), set up multiple cameras (wide, detail, mood), and iterate on composition until the scene tells a clear story. This week directly exercises all grading pillars: Technical Proficiency, Artistic Direction, Problem-Solving, and Professional Presentation.

Week 12: Technical Review, Artistic Refinement & Final Submission

This final week is about finishing like a pro: optimize meshes/materials/lighting for performance and cleanliness, add an artistic polish pass (composition, mood, focal points), render a multi-camera set, and assemble a professional submission package with clear documentation and a short presentation.
