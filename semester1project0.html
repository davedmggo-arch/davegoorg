Project Brief:

Description:
Choose one prop from your assigned room theme and draw three orthographic views:
  • Front view
  • Side view
  • Top view
This will serve as your blueprint when you later model the prop in Blender.

Deliverables:
  • At three orthographic views (front, side, top) of a single object.
  • Proper layer organization (construction, outlines, details).

Prop Choices (Assigned by Theme)
  • Woodland Kitchen: Mug, Plate, Candle in Holder, Lantern
  • Prince’s Bedroom: Jewelry Box, Picture Frame, Table Lamp, Vase
  • 1900s Workspace: Desk Lamp, Ceramic Cup with Saucer, Hammer and Nails, Ruler / Straightedge
  • Cozy Living Room: Bowl, Potted Plant, Throw Pillow, Decorative Sculpture

Guidelines
  • Keep the drawings clean and proportional.
  • Use simple lines — no shading required.
  • Focus on consistency between views (the object should read as the same item in all angles).
  • Think about design variation within your theme (e.g., a rustic wooden lantern vs. a futuristic glowing lantern).

Grading (20% of semester grade):

  • Technical Execution (40%) – Correct tool usage, file organization, layer management.
  • Accuracy & Proportion (30%) – Drawings match technical drawing standards, consistent between views.
  • Interface Understanding (20%) – Basic navigation, orthographic projection, keyframes if used.
  • Professional Presentation (10%) – Documentation, naming conventions, clarity of submission.

Weekly Breakdown

Week 1 – Digital Art Fundamentals & Grease Pencil Basics

Students are introduced to the importance of 3D tools across creative industries and the unique role Blender plays as an accessible, professional-grade software. They set up their tablets for pressure sensitivity, configure Blender’s Grease Pencil workspace, and learn core drawing shortcuts. Basic exercises include practicing line weight control, drawing simple shapes, organizing layers, and creating a clean file structure, giving students a solid foundation in digital art workflows.

Week 2 – Grease Pencil Tools & Technical Drawing

This week focuses on mastering Grease Pencil’s drawing tools and materials while developing technical drawing skills. Students practice creating multi-view orthographic projections of simple objects, maintaining consistent proportions, and applying proper line weight hierarchy. They also learn about materials, brush customization, and modifiers to enhance drawings. By the end, students produce accurate, well-organized technical drawings that demonstrate precision and clarity.

Week 3 – Animation Interface & Project Completion

Enter card content here.Students are introduced to Blender’s animation interface, learning how to use the timeline, keyframes, interpolation, and onion skinning. They apply this knowledge to create a simple animation while finalizing their multi-view technical drawings. The week is dedicated to polishing work, documenting their workflow, and preparing professional submissions. This marks the completion of Project 0, reinforcing Grease Pencil fundamentals while bridging into 3D modeling for the next project.
