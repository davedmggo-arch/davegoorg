Project 1: Environmental Tiles and Procedural Systems (Weeks 1-3)

In this project, you will move away from modeling individual props and toward building reusable environmental "tiles." You will design three distinct but aesthetically linked tiles. These tiles must function as a modular system where assets can be swapped between locations to ensure production efficiency.

Start Learning!!

Project Brief: The Environmental Tile

1. Project Scope and Themes

You must choose one of the following environment types to build your tile. Each tile must contain three distinct zones: a Road (traversable path), a Target Location (the focal point of interest), and a Non-Accessible Location (environmental boundaries).

  • Theme 1: The Dense Forest: Focus on organic verticality, leaf litter, and mossy boundaries.
  • Theme 2: The Rugged Shoreline: Focus on wet vs. dry surfaces, jagged rock formations, and tidal debris.
  • Theme 3: The Mountain Scenery: Focus on sheer vertical planes, scree slopes, and harsh silhouettes.

Reference Building Requirements

Before opening Blender, you must gather a "Funpack" of at least 10 high-quality images. These should include 4 images of real-world terrain, 3 images of your specific objects (rocks, trees, etc.), and 3 images of lighting moods. You will use these as constant guides throughout the process.

3. Cross-Theme Asset Mixing

One of the keys to efficiency is "Kit-Bashing" across environments. Consider these examples:

  • Forest + Mountain: Use your low-poly Mountain rocks as large cliff faces within the Forest tile to block the Non-Accessible zones.
  • Shoreline + Forest: Use the "Scattered Debris" logic from the forest floor to create piles of driftwood on your Shoreline path.
  • Mountain + Shoreline: Re-scale your large mountain peaks into small jagged rocks that sit in the surf of the Shoreline tile.

4. Deliverables and Naming Conventions

All files must follow the strict naming convention below. Incorrectly named files will result in a grade deduction.

Blender File: FirstName_LastName_ANI406_Project_001_v001.blend
3 JPEGs (3 different angles):

Final Check: Ensure your target location follows the Rule of Thirds and that your path clearly guides the viewer's eye. Every vertex must serve a purpose.
