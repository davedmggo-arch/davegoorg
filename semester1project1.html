Project 1: 3D Fundamentals (Weeks 4–7)

You will build a simple 3D scene composed of a character and environment made from primitive shapes. This project introduces shape language, silhouette design, and scene composition. You will also light and render your scene from multiple camera angles.

Deliverables:

  • A 3D character constructed from primitive shapes (cube, sphere, cylinder, etc.).
  • A basic environment that complements the character.
  • Three rendered views (different angles).
  • Documentation of modeling process and design choices.

Grading (30% of semester grade):

  • Technical Execution (40%) – Correct 3D modeling methods, clean topology, organized outliner.
  • Design Principles (30%) – Effective use of shape language, clear silhouette readability, strong composition.
  • Creativity (20%) – Originality in character/environment design and storytelling.
  • Presentation (10%) – Professional render output, documentation, scene naming and organization.

Week 4 – 3D Space Fundamentals & Table Composition

Students are introduced to 3D coordinate systems (global vs. local space) and learn to navigate effectively within Blender. They practice creating simple objects using primitive shapes by building a table and surrounding props, focusing on silhouette design, object placement, and scene readability. Navigation drills reinforce the use of views, shading modes, and selection tools to prepare for more complex modeling.

Week 5 – Shape Language & Object Detailing

This week explores how shape language (circles, squares, triangles) conveys meaning and personality in design. Students refine their table scene objects with Edit Mode tools such as extrude, inset, bevel, and loop cuts. They also practice proportional editing for organic curves and advanced transformations for precision. Exercises emphasize maintaining strong silhouettes while adding details that reinforce design intent.

Week 6 – Character Design & Basic Materials

Students block out a simple, stylized character using primitives, focusing on proportions and posing within the table scene. They are introduced to Blender’s material system, learning how to create and name materials properly, control properties like roughness and metallic values, and apply them consistently across their scene. Character materials (skin, fabric, hair) are integrated with table and props, ensuring visual harmony.

Week 7 – Environment Design & Project Completion

The final week of Project 1 covers environment storytelling, lighting, and camera setup. Students apply three-point lighting to establish mood, experiment with environmental elements to support their narrative, and compose scenes using camera angles and the rule of thirds. They finalize their project by polishing models, refining materials, and rendering multiple views of their complete table scene with character and props, preparing for submission.
