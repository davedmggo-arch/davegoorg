Project 1: "The Essential Object" (Weeks 1-4)

You are to create a functional table that houses various props specific to your chosen theme and target audience. The table should be designed to complement and make sense with the props it will hold. This project will introduce you to basic modeling techniques while developing your understanding of how objects relate to each other in a thematic environment. You will learn to use funpacks - collections of reference materials provided by the instructor that will guide your creative process.

Project Brief

Choose 3-4 thematic props from a room theme of your choice on top of a table. Create and assemble each object and render them in 3 different arrangements.

Theme Options:
Children's Fantasy: Enchanted forest workshop where woodland creatures create magical items
  • Glowing acorns, fairy dust jars, miniature mushroom houses, talking stones
Teen Sci-Fi: Future classroom where students learn about advanced technology and alien cultures
  • Holographic textbooks, alien artifacts, data crystals, molecular models
Young Adult Urban: Underground artist's studio where creative expression thrives in a hidden space
  • Spray paint cans, sketchbooks, stencil templates, mixed media art supplies
Adult Historical: Early 1900s parlor or study room with period-appropriate furnishings and objects
  • Brass telescope, leather-bound books, fountain pen set, vintage photograph frame

Deliverables:
  • 3D model of a functional table
  • 3D models of 3-4 thematic props
  • Basic renders showing props arranged on the table
  • Funpack package of table with props

    Weekly Breakdown


    Week 1: Getting Started with Blender
    This week, you'll take your first steps into the world of 3D by learning Blender's interface and fundamental navigation tools. You'll discover how to move around in 3D space, create and manipulate basic shapes, and understand the coordinate system that makes 3D possible. By the end of the week, you'll have created your first simple 3D object and begun collecting reference materials for your projects, setting the foundation for your journey into 3D visual development.
    Week 2: Creating Your First Props
    This week, you'll dive into the exciting world of 3D modeling by creating your first thematic props. You'll learn how to work in Edit mode, use essential modeling tools like extrusion and beveling, and understand why good topology matters. By the end of the week, you'll have created 2-3 props that not only look good but also serve a narrative purpose in your chosen theme, taking your first big step from simple shapes to meaningful 3D objects.
    Week 3: Perfecting Your Props and Table
    This week, you'll refine your modeling skills by adding professional-level details to your props and creating a complementary table design. You'll learn advanced techniques for optimizing edge flow, analyzing silhouettes, and using modifiers to enhance your models. By the end of the week, you'll have a cohesive set of themed props and table that work together visually and narratively, demonstrating your growing understanding of form, function, and design consistency.
    Week 4: Finalizing Your Set and Learning to Render
    This week, you'll bring your first project to completion by learning how to apply materials, set up lighting, and create professional renders of your work. You'll discover how to arrange your props on your table for maximum narrative impact and learn the fundamentals of camera composition.
