Week 2: Creating Your First Props

This week, you'll dive into the exciting world of 3D modeling by creating your first thematic props. You'll learn how to work in Edit mode, use essential modeling tools like extrusion and beveling, and understand why good topology matters. By the end of the week, you'll have created 2-3 props that not only look good but also serve a narrative purpose in your chosen theme, taking your first big step from simple shapes to meaningful 3D objects.

What You'll Learn

  • How to work with Edit mode and different selection methods
  • Techniques for extruding, beveling, and subdividing your models
  • The basics of edge flow and why good topology matters
  • How to use Boolean operations to combine or cut shapes
  • Applying reference materials to make your props more realistic
  • Introduction to powerful tools like Subdivision Surface and Mirror modifiers

Key Concepts & Tools


Edit Mode Operations
  • Tab: Switch between Edit and Object mode
  • 1, 2, 3: Switch between selecting Vertices, Edges, and Faces
  • Ctrl + Tab: Quick menu for selecting different modes
  • Alt + Click: Select an entire edge or face loop
  • Shift + Alt + Click: Add or remove loops from your selection
  • Ctrl + L: Select all connected parts of your model
  • L: Select parts connected to where your cursor is
  • Ctrl + I: Invert your selection (select everything you haven't selected)
  • H: Hide selected parts (great for focusing on specific areas)
  • Alt + H: Unhide everything you've hidden
Modeling Tools
  • E: Extrude (pull new geometry from existing faces)
  • Ctrl + R: Add edge loops (cuts across your model)
  • Ctrl + B: Bevel edges (round off sharp corners)
  • I: Inset faces (create inset faces within existing ones)
  • K: Knife tool (make custom cuts)
  • F: Fill faces or bridge edges
  • Ctrl + F: Special face operations menu
  • V: Rip vertices (split them apart)
  • Y: Split edges
  • P: Separate selected parts into new objects
  • M: Merge vertices, edges, or faces together
Advanced Selection Techniques
  • Ctrl + +: Expand your selection
  • Ctrl + -: Shrink your selection
  • Shift + G: Select similar elements
  • Ctrl + Left Mouse: Select the shortest path between two points
  • Ctrl + Shift + Left Mouse: Select an entire area
Modifier System
  • Ctrl + A: Apply transformations or modifiers
  • M: Add a modifier
  • Ctrl + 1, 2, 3: Add subdivision surface modifier
  • Ctrl + 0: Disable subdivision modifier
  • Alt + M: Add mirror modifier
Boolean Operations
  • Ctrl + Shift + B: Add boolean modifier
  • Difference: Cut one shape out of another
  • Union: Merge two shapes together
  • Intersection: Keep only where shapes overlap
  • Cleaning up after boolean operations (they can create messy geometry)
Edge Flow and Topology
  • Understanding edge loops and how they help with deformation
  • Maintaining quad-based topology (four-sided polygons)
  • Avoiding triangles and n-gons in areas that will bend or deform
  • Creating edge flow that supports the form and function of your model
Tips and Tricks
  • See Your Edge Flow Clearly: Enable edge loop visualization in viewport overlays (Ctrl+Alt+Q) to see how your edges flow across your model. This helps you spot topology issues before they become problems.
  • Use the Mirror Modifier for Symmetry: Always use the mirror modifier for symmetrical objects. Make sure your object's origin is in the center (Ctrl+Alt+Shift+C) before applying the modifier for perfect symmetry.
  • Work Smart with Subdivision Surface: Add a subdivision surface modifier early but keep it at level 1 while modeling. Use support loops (Ctrl+R near edges) to maintain your shape's integrity when you add more subdivisions.

Homework

  • Create Your Thematic Props: Build 2-3 props based on your chosen theme and reference materials. Focus on clean topology and proper edge flow. Submit both wireframe and solid views so we can see your modeling technique.
  • Experiment with Modifiers: Apply at least 3 different modifiers to your props (Subdivision Surface, Mirror, Bevel). Document how each modifier affects your model and explain why you chose each one.
  • Analyze Your Topology: Submit screenshots highlighting the edge flow on your models with annotations explaining your topology decisions. Why did you place edges where you did?
