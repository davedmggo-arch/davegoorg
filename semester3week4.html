Tips and Tricks
  • Build a Material Library: Create a simple material library with common materials (wood, metal, plastic) that you can reuse across projects. This saves time and ensures consistency in your work.
  • Build Your Lighting Step by Step: Start with a single key light, then add fill lights, and finally add rim lights. Render after each addition to understand how each light contributes to your scene.
  • Save Important Camera Views: Use camera bookmarks (Ctrl + B in camera view) to save important angles. This allows you to quickly compare different compositions without losing your favorite views.
  • Render in Layers: Render different elements (props, table, background) on separate layers. This gives you more control in post-processing and allows for quick adjustments without re-rendering everything.

Homework

  • Create Final Renders: Produce 3-4 final renders showing your props arranged on the table. Include different camera angles and lighting setups. Submit high-quality images that showcase your work.
  • Explore Material Variations: Apply at least 3 different material variations to your set. Submit renders showing each variation and explain the mood or feeling each material combination creates.
  • Experiment with Lighting: Create 2 different lighting setups for your scene (e.g., daytime and nighttime). Submit renders and explain how lighting affects the narrative and mood of your scene.
